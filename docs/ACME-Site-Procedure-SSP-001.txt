Revision: 3
Date: January 2025
Classification: Controlled Document

---

1. INTRODUCTION AND SCOPE

This procedure applies to all ACME Mining Co. personnel, contractors, and visitors operating at the ACME Open Cut Mine. It establishes the minimum safety requirements for all site activities.

1.1 Purpose
To ensure the health, safety, and welfare of all persons at the ACME Mine site in accordance with legislative requirements.

1.2 Definitions
- PPE: Personal Protective Equipment
- JSEA: Job Safety and Environment Analysis
- SWP: Safe Work Procedure
- Competent Person: A person who has acquired through training, qualification or experience the knowledge and skills to perform a task.

---

2. PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE)

2.1 Mandatory PPE for All Personnel
All personnel on site must wear the following minimum PPE at all times when outside of enclosed structures:
- Hard hat (AS/NZS 1801 compliant)
- Safety boots with steel cap and penetration-resistant sole
- High-visibility vest or shirt (Class D/N)
- Safety glasses

2.2 Task-Specific PPE
Additional PPE may be required for specific tasks. Supervisors must identify task-specific PPE in the JSEA.

2.3 PPE Inspection
Personnel are responsible for inspecting their own PPE regularly. Damaged or defective PPE must be removed from service and reported to a supervisor.

---

3. WORKING AT HEIGHTS

3.1 Scope
This section applies to all work performed at a height greater than 2 metres where there is risk of fall.

3.2 Fall Prevention
Fall prevention systems (guardrails, scaffolding) are preferred over fall arrest systems. A JSEA must be completed before commencing any work at heights.

3.3 Harness Inspections
Safety harnesses must be inspected before each use by the user. Harnesses showing signs of wear or damage must not be used.

3.4 Rescue Plan
A rescue plan must be in place before work at heights begins. The plan must be reviewed with all personnel involved.

---

4. EXCAVATION AND TRENCHING

4.1 Permit Requirements
All excavations must be authorised by an Excavation Permit before work commences. The permit must identify underground services and exclusion zones.

4.2 Battering and Shoring
Excavations deeper than 1.5m must be battered to a safe angle or shored to prevent collapse. A competent person must assess the soil conditions.

4.3 Barricading
All excavations must be barricaded when unattended. Barricades must be a minimum of 1 metre from the edge of the excavation.

---

5. VEHICLE AND MOBILE PLANT INTERACTION

5.1 Exclusion Zones
Pedestrians must not enter active mobile plant operating areas without a positive communication with the operator.

5.2 Speed Limits
The maximum speed limit on haul roads is 50 km/h. Speed limits in workshop areas are 10 km/h. Speed limits must be observed at all times.

5.3 Pre-start Checks
All vehicles and mobile plant must undergo a documented pre-start check before operation each shift.

---

6. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

6.1 Emergency Contact
In the event of an emergency, contact the Site Emergency Coordinator on radio Channel 9.

6.2 Muster Points
All personnel must be familiar with designated muster points. Muster points are shown on the site map posted at site entrances.

6.3 Incident Reporting
All incidents, near misses, and hazards must be reported immediately to the supervising officer using Form ACME-IR-001.
